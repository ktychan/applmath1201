## Course Learning Outcomes

Applied Mathematics 1201, Calculus & Probability with Biological Applications, discuss mathematical modelling of natural phenomenons and analytic techniques of mathematical models with examples mostly chosen from biology.  The main classes of mathematical objects are 
1. power models {math}`y = c x^{\alpha}`,
1. recurrence,
1. ordinary differential equations (ODE),
1. systems of recurrences and ODEs, 
1. probability, and discrete and continuous random variables,
1. matricies, eigenvectors and eigenvalues (with complex numbers).

Upon the completion of the course, you will be able to do the followings:

- Describe natural phenomenons using power, discrete-time and continuous-time models.
- Describe uncertainty using probability and random variables.
- Discern appropriate applications of discrete-time versus continuous-time models.
- Discrete-time models.
    - Describe natural phenomenon using recurrences.
    - Solve linear recurrences.
    - Find parameters in a linear recurrence satisfying a given condition.
    - Describe the long-term behaviour of systems of linear recurrences.
- Continuous-time models.
    - Understand the exponential-growth, logistic models and the Lokta-Volterra models.
    - Use ordinary differential equations to model mixing problems.

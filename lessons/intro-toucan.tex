\section{A toucan of truth?}
\begin{example} \label{ex:toucan}
  In this example, work in small teams to evaluate scientific arguments. Carefully read through the four parts of this activity: \emph{the setup}, \emph{the data}, \emph{the question} and \emph{the arguments}. Finally, discuss within your group which intern's argument you choose to accept.

  \begin{enumerate}[wide, label=(\arabic*)]
    \item \emph{The setup}. An ecologist studied birds in Costa Rica and recorded data relating sizes (in centimetres) of birds and their mass (in grams). 
      Sizes of birds are challenging to quantify due to their complex geometries. The ecologist makes a \emph{simplification} and treats birds as if they were perfect cubes. Define the \hlmain{length} \(\ell\) of a bird to be the side length of the smallest perfect cube containing the bird.

      \begin{figure}[H]
        \centering
        \includegraphics[width=1.6in]{../standalones/image-bird-cube.png}
      \end{figure}

      \clearpage

    \item \emph{The data}. Explore the ecologist's data on the following plots.

      \centerline{
        \includestandalone[page=1]{../standalones/plot-birds-of-costa-rica}
        \includestandalone[page=2]{../standalones/plot-birds-of-costa-rica}
        \includestandalone[page=3]{../standalones/plot-birds-of-costa-rica}
      }

    \item \emph{The question}. The ecologist does not have data on toucans and is \emph{interested} in this question:
      \begin{center}
        \hlmain{What is the mass of an adult toucan given that its length is roughly 40 cm?}
      \end{center}

    \item \emph{The arguments}. Two interns put forth their analysis. 
      \begin{enumerate}[wide, label=Intern \Alph*:]
        \item A cubic model \(m = 0.0061 \ell^{3}\) fits the data.
        \item A quadratic model \(m = 0.25 \ell^{2}\) also fits the data.
      \end{enumerate}

      \includestandalone[page=4]{../standalones/plot-birds-of-costa-rica}
      \includestandalone[page=5]{../standalones/plot-birds-of-costa-rica}
  \end{enumerate}

  \medskip
  \begin{mdframed}[roundcorner=10pt]
    \textbf{Your turn!} Interestingly, both arguments lead to remarkably similar predictions that the mass of an adult toucan is roughly \(400\) grams.
    \begin{center}
      \hlmain{\faComment{} You are the ecologist. How do you evaluate your interns' arguments?}
    \end{center}

    Apply dimensional homogeneity and log-log transformation to support your arguments.
  \end{mdframed}
\end{example}
